  • Inquire for proper certification and insurance paperwork. Legitimate locksmiths should happily provide proof of a legitimate license to operate in your location and valid insurance coverage. Verify this information with the relevant authorities.
  • Check professional certifications. Locksmiths certified by organizations like the Associated Locksmiths of America (ALOA) have participated in rigorous training and adhere to a strict code of ethics, ensuring high-quality skills and reliability.
  • Beware for locksmiths without a physical address or storefront. Reputable companies should have a verifiable physical address and not operate solely from a temporary setup.

  • Insist proper identification from the locksmith who comes at your premises. A legitimate locksmith should possess company-issued identification and be willing to provide it upon request.
