  • Ask for proper licensing and insurance paperwork. Legitimate locksmiths should readily provide proof of a valid license to operate in your state and valid insurance coverage. Verify this information with the relevant authorities.
  • Check professional certifications. Locksmiths certified by organizations like the Associated Locksmiths of America (ALOA) have participated in rigorous training and adhere to a strict standard of practice, ensuring high-quality skills and reliability.
  • Watch out for locksmiths without a physical address or storefront. Reputable companies should have a verifiable business location and not operate solely from a temporary setup.
